What You Need to Know About Chicago’s New Yard Waste Removal System

  • Yard waste will either be collected directly from your home or from a designated drop-off area in your community depending on the month.
  • Small fines will be given to those who improperly dispose of waste (for example, if yard waste or recyclables are found in the garbage).
  • Communities with the highest yard waste collection rate will receive a monetary bonus for park district/community park improvements. The amount of waste will be weighted according to the number of trees and greenery in each community.
  • For further information, please visit www.chilawndisposal.com.

Collection Information

  • May – November

    1. Purchase a reusable bin or brown recyclable bags.
    2. Put yard waste (refer to table below) into bin/bag.
    3. To avoid fines, be sure to sort garbage, recyclables, and yard waste in the appropriate bins.
    4. Place yard waste bin/bag directly next to your garbage and recycle bins.
    5. The City of Chicago will remove and compost your yard waste on the assigned pick-up day.

  • May – August:

    Pick-up every two weeks.

  • September-November:

    Pick-up every week.

  • December-April

    1. Purchase a brown recyclable bag.
    2. Put yard waste (refer to table below) into bag.
    3. To avoid fines, be sure to sort garbage, recyclables, and yard waste in the appropriate bins.
    4. Bring bag to the designated drop-off area in your community.
    5. The City of Chicago will remove and compost your yard waste on the assigned pick-up day every two weeks.

What is yard waste?

  • Leaves
  • Grass clippings
  • Branches, brush, clippings from shrubbery
  • Garden waste
  • Kitchen scraps (such as fruit and vegetable scraps, egg shells, and coffee grounds)

What is not yard waste?

  • Dirt, rocks, or gravel
  • Animal waste
  • Plastic bags
  • Plant ties/netting
  • Garden tools
  • Anything else that should be placed in a garbage bin or recycled
